SUMMARY
13
This bill is reported out by the Joint Standing Committee on Judiciary to implement 
14
statutory changes recommended by the Right to Know Advisory Committee.  The joint 
15
standing committee has not taken a position on the substance of this bill.  By reporting this 
16
bill out, the joint standing committee is not suggesting and does not intend to suggest that 
17
it agrees or disagrees with any aspect of this bill; instead, the committee is reporting the 
18
bill out for the sole purpose of having a bill printed that can be referred to the committee 
19
for an appropriate public hearing and subsequent processing in the normal course.  The 
20
joint standing committee is taking this action to ensure clarity and transparency in the 
21
legislative review of the proposals contained in the bill.
22

23

24

25
Title 5, section 25007, subsection 1, paragraph A to carry out research necessary to 
26
determine the status of historically disadvantaged racial, indigenous and tribal populations, 
27
except that the executive director may authorize disclosure of personally identifiable 
28

29
the personally identifiable information applies.
