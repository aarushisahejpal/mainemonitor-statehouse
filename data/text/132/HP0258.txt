§378-B.  Lottery winner identity; confidentiality
4
The identity of a person awarded a prize of $500,000 or more is confidential unless the 
5
person awarded the prize provides written authorization to the bureau allowing the 
6
disclosure of that person's identity.  The bureau may disclose the identity of the person to 
7
another state agency solely for purposes authorized under this chapter, and that state agency 
8
is prohibited from further disclosing the person's identity.
9
Information designated as confidential under this section is not a public record as 
10
defined in Title 1, section 402, subsection 3.
11
SUMMARY
12
This bill provides that the identity of a person awarded a lottery prize of $500,000 or 
13
more is confidential unless the person awarded the prize provides written authorization to 
14
the Department of Administrative and Financial Services, Bureau of Alcoholic Beverages 
15
and Lottery Operations allowing the disclosure of that person's identity.  The bill authorizes 
16
the bureau to disclose the identity of the person to another state agency for purposes 
17
authorized under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 8, chapter 14-A and the bill prohibits 
18
that state agency from further disclosing the person's identity.
