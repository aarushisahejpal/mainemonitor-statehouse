Sec. 1.  Maine Promise Initiative.  Resolved: That the Department of Education 
2
shall create a program to be known as the Maine Promise Initiative, referred to in this 
3
resolve as "the initiative."  The primary goal of the initiative is to have, by 2030, at least 
4
95% of the State's secondary school students graduate from high school in either a 4-year 
5
or a 6-year time period.  The initiative must include additional 2030 graduation rate goals, 
6
including increasing graduation rates for identified populations that are below the 2025 
7
graduation rates of the general population of secondary school students.  As used in this 
8
resolve, "identified populations" includes, but is not limited to, students who are unhoused, 
9
Native American students, students in the child welfare system and students in the juvenile 
10
justice system.  The department shall convene a working group of interested stakeholders 
11
to develop a strategic plan to implement the initiative and achieve its goals.  The department 
12
shall report on its progress in developing the strategic plan and implementing the initiative 
13
and its recommendations, including any suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing 
14
Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs by December 3, 2025.  The joint standing 
15
committee may report out a bill to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.

SUMMARY
24
This resolve directs the Department of Education to create a program to be known as 
25
the Maine Promise Initiative.  The primary goal of the initiative is to have, by 2030, at least 
26
95% of the State's secondary school students graduate from high school in either a 4-year 
27
or a 6-year time period.  The initiative must include additional 2030 graduation rate goals, 
28
including increasing graduation rates for identified populations that are below the 2025 
29
graduation rates of the general population of secondary school students.  The department 
30
is directed to report to the Joint Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs by 
31
December 3, 2025.  The joint standing committee is authorized to report out a bill to the 
32
Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.  The resolve includes a one-time 
33
appropriation of $100,000 in fiscal year 2025-26 to support the creation of the initiative.
