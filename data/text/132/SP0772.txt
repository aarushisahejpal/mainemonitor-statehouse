Sec. 2.  Court navigation program plan. Resolved:  That the task force shall 
26
create a court navigation program plan.  In developing the plan, the task force shall examine 
27
court navigation programs nationwide and recommend a model for a court navigation 
28
program for use in this State.  The court navigation program must provide guidance and 
29
information about court processes and procedures to persons involved with the court 
30
system.  The court navigation program also must refer persons involved with the court 
31
system to community-based resources related to their behavioral health needs, such as 
32
mental health treatment and substance use disorder treatment, and their economic and 
33
social service-related needs, such as housing, employment, transportation and food, in 
34
order to address those needs.  The task force shall consider experiential learning 
35
opportunities for social work students and law students who assist in the court navigation 
36
program to earn academic credit.
37

40

SUMMARY
6
This resolve directs the University of Maine System to convene a task force to create 
7
a court navigation program plan.  In developing the plan, the task force must examine court 
8
navigation programs nationwide and recommend a model for a court navigation program 
9
for use in this State.  The court navigation program must provide guidance and information 
10
about court processes and procedures to persons involved with the court system.  The court 
11
navigation program also must refer persons involved with the court system to community-
12
based resources related to their behavioral health needs, such as mental health treatment 
13
and substance use disorder treatment, and their economic and social service-related needs, 
14
such as housing, employment, transportation and food, in order to address those needs.  The 
15
task force must consider experiential learning opportunities for social work students and 
16
law students who assist in the court navigation program to earn academic credit.  No later 
17
than December 3, 2025, the task force must submit a report that includes the court 
18
navigation program plan, including a model for a court navigation program for use in this 
19
State, and that includes suggested legislation to the Joint Standing Committee on Judiciary.  
20
The joint standing committee may report out legislation based on the subject matter of the 
21
report to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.
