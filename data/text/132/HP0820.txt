§3207.  Working Waterfront Information Campaign Fund
4
The Working Waterfront Information Campaign Fund, referred to in this section as 
5
"the fund," is established.  Balances in the fund may not lapse and must be carried forward 
6
and used for the purposes of this section.  The fund is administered by the office, and the 
7
office may accept and deposit in the fund money from private and public sources.  In 
8
carrying out the purposes of the fund, the office shall work with the Department of 
9
Economic and Community Development, Office of Tourism; coastal municipal and 
10
regional governments; and organizations and coalitions that have experience advocating 
11
for the protection and preservation of working waterfronts in the State and experience 
12
working with persons who use working waterfronts in the State. In collaboration with the 
13
entities listed in this section, the office shall use the fund in the production of working 
14
waterfront information campaigns, including the design and implementation of an online 
15
and direct mail information campaign targeting tourists and new residents in coastal and 
16
island communities, to provide information about what working waterfronts are, who uses 
17
working waterfronts and opportunities to protect and preserve working waterfronts.  As 
18
used in this section, "working waterfront" means a parcel of land abutting water subject to 
19
tidal influence or land located in the intertidal zone that is used primarily or predominantly 
20
to provide access to or support the conduct of commercial fishing and marine activities.
21
SUMMARY
22
This bill establishes the Working Waterfront Information Campaign Fund, which is 
23
administered by the Maine Office of Community Affairs.  The office is directed to work 
24
with the Department of Economic and Community Development, Office of Tourism; 
25
coastal municipal and regional governments; and organizations and coalitions that have 
26
experience advocating for the protection and preservation of working waterfronts in the 
27
State and experience working with persons who use working waterfronts in the State.  In 
28
collaboration with those entities, the office is directed to produce working waterfront 
29
information campaigns.
